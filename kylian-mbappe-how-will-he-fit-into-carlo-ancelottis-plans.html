Kylian Mbappe: How Will He Fit Into Carlo Ancelotti's Plans?

Kylian Mbappe has signed with Real Madrid after leaving PSG at the end of his contract. The 25-year-old joins a star-studded attack at the European champions, featuring Vinicius Jr, Rodrygo, and Jude Bellingham. But how will the Frenchman integrate into the team?

Jude Bellingham slipped in Vinicius Jr to score and Real Madrid's key forward pair had done it. A Champions League trophy delivered without a recognised, world-class No 9 in the team.

Kylian Mbappe arrives at Real Madrid after previously turning down the Spanish giants three times: once after a trial with their U12 team, and twice to stay with PSG in 2017 and 2022. Now, he joins the Bernabeu when the team needs him most.

"If he was to come and give us that, we would be in a really great place to go and take us to another level. One of the best players in the world!"

Ancelotti adapted to the loss of Benzema by not directly replacing him, instead using a 4-3-1-2 formation with Vinicius and Rodrygo as 'split forwards' in wide areas, allowing new signing Bellingham to play a free role at the tip of a midfield diamond.

This front three - Vinicius, Rodrygo, and Bellingham - has been crucial, with Vinicius and Bellingham scoring 24 and 23 goals respectively this season. Rodrygo, despite transfer rumors, is committed to staying in Madrid next season.

Mbappe, with his 256 goals in 308 games for PSG, is set to become the jewel in Real's crown. But how will he fit into a team where Vinicius, who also plays on the left, is a key player?

"Vinicius has really made the left-hand side his. That is his best position," Spanish football journalist Semra Hunter told Sky Sports News.

Assuming Ancelotti sticks with a 4-4-2 next season, with Bellingham at the top of the midfield diamond, Vinicius and Mbappe could form a two-pronged attack similar to the Vinicius-Rodrygo partnership.

Replacing Rodrygo, who has been vital in Real's recent Champions League successes, is a tough decision for Ancelotti. Rodrygo's versatility, able to play on the right, left, or as a No 10, makes him an asset.

Ancelotti could consider a 4-3-3 formation, with Mbappe in the middle and Vinicius and Rodrygo on the wings. However, this would require sacrificing a midfielder, which might not be feasible given Real's depth in that area.

Mbappe isn't the only new arrival; Brazilian starlet Endrick adds another option. Despite his potential, Endrick will likely develop over time while the spotlight remains on Mbappe, who is expected to help Real Madrid dominate Europe.

If Real Madrid can win Champions League titles without him, the addition of Mbappe could elevate the team's achievements even further.
