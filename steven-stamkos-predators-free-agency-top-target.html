Steven Stamkos Predators’ Free Agency Top Target

In a swift move just fifteen minutes into NHL free agency, the Nashville Predators emerged as the day’s standout team. They secured agreements with three top-tier players: center Steven Stamkos, right winger Jonathan Marchessault, and defenseman Brady Skjei.

These signings bolster the Predators' top six with two 40-goal scorers and add a seasoned top-four defenseman known for his potent shot and offensive contributions from the blue line.

Stamkos, a two-time winner of the Rocket Richard Trophy for NHL's top scorer, comes off a stellar 2023 season with 40 goals and 81 points. The 34-year-old forward boasts a seven-time All-Star record and two Stanley Cup victories. Since his debut in 2008, Stamkos ranks second only to Alex Ovechkin in goals scored, leads in shooting percentage (16.7%), ranks second in power-play goals (214) and game-winners (85), and sits third in points (1,137) and power-play points (422).

Marchessault, 33, joins from the Vegas Golden Knights, where he tallied a career-high 42 goals and 69 points last season. His consistency includes seven seasons with 20+ goals and six with 50+ points. Marchessault's achievements include winning the Stanley Cup in 2022 and being named Conn Smythe Trophy recipient as the postseason MVP, becoming the 10th undrafted player to earn the honor.

Skjei, 30, arrives from the Carolina Hurricanes with his strongest seasons to date, scoring a career-best 18 goals and 38 points in 2022, followed by 13 goals and a career-high 47 points last season.

In addition to these acquisitions, Nashville bid farewell to forwards Jason Zucker, who signed a one-year, $5 million deal with the Buffalo Sabres, and Kiefer Sherwood, who inked a two-year, $3 million contract with the Vancouver Canucks.
