Tyreek Hill 'Excited to See Where I Fit' in WR Contracts

Tyreek Hill is optimistic about the evolving wide receiver market.

After an offseason training session, the eight-time Pro Bowler shared his thoughts on the rising salaries for elite wide receivers and his place in this trend.

"I'm very excited to be part of the old wave, which was $30 million, and then Justin Jefferson came and surpassed that," Hill said, according to Aaron Wilson of KPRC Houston. "I'm very proud of those guys, and happy for my teammate Waddle getting his new deal. For players like me, that's great. I'm 30 years old, also looking for a new deal. So, I'm very excited to see where I fit into that category. It's amazing."

Hill signed a four-year, $120 million extension with the Miami Dolphins in 2022, setting an average annual value above $30 million per season and contributing to the surge in wide receiver contracts.

Since then, players like Justin Jefferson and A.J. Brown have secured deals surpassing Hill's salary. As Hill approaches free agency in 2027, the market will likely continue to grow, but his age might impact his long-term prospects.

However, Hill remains at the top of his game. He recorded 119 catches for 1,799 yards and 13 touchdowns in the 2023-24 season, earning first-team All-Pro honors, finishing sixth in MVP voting, and being the runner-up for the Offensive Player of the Year award.

While contract discussions might not be his primary focus heading into 2024, Hill aims to continue his impressive performance for Miami.
