Boston Celtics Secure Record 18th NBA Title

Boston Celtics head coach Joe Mazzulla lifts the Larry O'Brien Championship Trophy high at TD Garden.

The Boston Celtics have claimed a record 18th NBA title by defeating the Dallas Mavericks in a 4-1 series victory.

After a heavy loss in game four in Dallas, the Celtics returned to Boston and delivered a decisive 106-88 win.

The victory coincides with the anniversary of the Celtics' last championship win in 2008, moving them ahead of rivals, the Los Angeles Lakers, for the most championships.

The Celtics started strong, leading 28-18 in the first quarter and extending their lead to 67-46 by halftime. Although the Mavericks narrowed the gap slightly in the third quarter, the Celtics maintained control in the final quarter.

"We owed our fans, it's been a long journey," said Jayson Tatum, who contributed 31 points, 11 assists, and eight rebounds.

Jaylen Brown added 21 points and was named the NBA Finals MVP. Jrue Holiday scored 15 points and secured 11 rebounds, while center Kristaps Porzingis returned from injury to add five points.

At 35, Joe Mazzulla became the youngest coach to win a championship since Bill Russell led the Celtics in 1969. Meanwhile, 38-year-old Al Horford celebrated his first title after an NBA record 186 playoff games without one.

Luka Doncic led Dallas with 28 points, while Kyrie Irving managed 15 points against his former team.
