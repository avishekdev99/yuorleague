Our ‘24 Draft Prediction

Change is constant in the world of hockey. Just days after the Panthers claimed the Stanley Cup in an epic seven-game series against the Oilers, the league and its fans are already gearing up for the 2024 NHL Draft on Friday night. Each year, this event symbolizes hope for all 32 teams in the league's future. While some teams may swing and miss, others could discover a hidden gem that propels their franchise to greater heights.

This year's draft promises to be unusually unpredictable, with experts largely agreeing only on Macklin Celebrini going first overall. The rest of the draft remains a mystery, but what's draft season without a mock draft? Check out ours below.

1 – Macklin Celebrini

San Jose Sharks The only sure thing in this draft, Macklin Celebrini solidified his status as the top prospect this year with an outstanding freshman season at Boston University. He racked up 64 points in 38 games, becoming the youngest player to win the Hobey Baker Award. Celebrini joins forces with 2023's 4th overall pick, Will Smith, in San Jose's quest to rejuvenate their Western Conference standings.

2 – Ivan Demidov

Chicago Blackhawks Chicago's second overall pick immediately spices up the draft. Despite their roster needs everywhere, they opt for Demidov, a talented Russian winger who could potentially partner with Connor Bedard in the near future. While Demidov still has a year left on his contract with St. Petersburg, Chicago is in no hurry to rush him if it means securing the most talented player available.

3 – Artyom Levshunov

Anaheim Ducks With the third pick, the Ducks play it safe by selecting Artyom Levshunov from Michigan State. This 18-year-old defenseman promises years of excellence for Anaheim, showcasing reliability across the ice. With a burgeoning roster of young talent, Levshunov's addition bolsters the Ducks' lineup. In his debut NCAA season, he scored 9 goals and assisted 26 times in 38 games for the Spartans.

4 – Cayden Lindstrom

Columbus Blue Jackets Expected to be the first WHL player off the board, Lindstrom brings immense potential alongside some injury concerns that caused him to miss half of the previous season. Nonetheless, his impressive 27 goals in 32 games before injury make him a worthwhile risk for teams like Columbus.

5 – Tij Iginla

Montreal Canadiens With David Reinbacher and Lane Hutson already making waves as future stars on Montreal's blue line, the Canadiens shift focus to forward. Though Lindstrom and Demidov are strong contenders if available, Montreal's mock selection lands on Tij Iginla. Son of the legendary Jarome Iginla, Tij shined with 84 points in 64 games for the Kelowna Rockets this season, culminating in a fifth overall pick by Montreal.

6 – Anton Silayev

Utah Hockey Club Last year, Utah picked two towering Russians in Dmitry Simashev and Daniil But. This year, they repeat the trend with Silayev, an 18-year-old defenseman standing tall at 6'7". With Utah's back end needing reinforcement (with only five rostered defensemen and none signed for next season), Silayev offers hope for a promising defensive duo in the near future.

7 – Zeev Buium

Ottawa Senators In the second of four consecutive defenseman picks in our mock draft, Zeev Buium lands seventh overall to the Senators. The Denver University standout became only the third defenseman in the past two decades to score fifty points in his freshman season, showcasing both offensive prowess and defensive prowess. Buium fits perfectly with Ottawa's young and dynamic roster.

8 – Zayne Parekh

Seattle Kraken Seattle's defensive prospect pool requires a boost, making Parekh an ideal choice. Offensively gifted with 96 points in 66 games in the OHL this season, Parekh earned the CHL's top defenseman accolade and has drawn comparisons to Erik Karlsson. Though his defensive skills are a point of concern, Parekh's natural goal-scoring instincts make him a valuable long-term investment for Seattle.

9 – Sam Dickinson

Calgary Flames Calgary's draft flexibility allows them to select the best available player in Dickinson. The London standout defenseman amassed 70 points in 68 games last season, standing at 6'3". Dickinson is viewed as a reliable choice capable of handling significant minutes and contributing across special teams—a solid pick for Calgary's ongoing rebuild.

10 – Cole Eiserman

New Jersey Devils Eiserman's draft stock has fluctuated over the past year, but his prowess as one of the draft's premier goal-scorers remains evident. With a stacked roster, New Jersey can afford to patiently develop this American sniper. Eiserman's potential to refine his game and thrive among skilled linemates sets the stage for potential future success, possibly alongside Jack Hughes.
