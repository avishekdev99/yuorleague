MLS Manager Praises Celtic Hero’s Reputation in Canada

Alistair Johnston had an outstanding season with Celtic and is currently enjoying a productive summer with his national team. The right-back played a crucial role in Canada’s performance at the Copa America, helping them secure a 0-0 draw against Chile, which advanced them to the tournament's quarter-finals.

Johnston, a proud Canadian, consistently gives his all for the national team, a dedication that hasn’t gone unnoticed. He is a beloved figure among his teammates and supporters, known for his combative style that translates well to international football.

Former Canada manager and current Toronto FC boss John Herdman highlighted Johnston's esteemed reputation in his homeland. Herdman remarked:

“We always felt he was made for British football. Those who worked with him, including my defensive coach Jason de Vos, sensed he was a hard-nosed, high-intensity defender. He loves a tackle and enjoys getting forward to deliver crosses.

“He’s a proper Scottish football type. I’ve never seen Ali have a bad game for Canada. Certainly not in my time. He consistently performed at an eight out of 10 or higher. His mindset and character are built on solid habits.

“The only time he’s been out of sorts was in the Nations League Final when he was carrying an injury from Celtic. He probably returned too early to try and help us win. He said, ‘I’m sorry, gaffer,’ but I told him he should never be sorry because his effort is always there. Even then, he was still a seven out of 10.”

Despite having only made 62 appearances for Celtic, Johnston has already won five trophies, making a significant impact on the team's right flank. Initially brought in by Ange Postecoglou, he remains an important member of the squad under Brendan Rodgers.

Celtic is not expected to recruit a new right-back this summer, ensuring Johnston will continue to be the first choice next season. Returning to Scotland full of confidence after his Copa America exploits would be fantastic for the team. An extended stay in the tournament following the upcoming quarter-final on Friday night would further solidify his reputation as one of Canada’s finest and most consistent performers.
