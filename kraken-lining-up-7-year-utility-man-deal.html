Kraken Lining Up 7 Year Utility Man Deal

The Kraken are set to finalize a seven-year contract with free agent center Chandler Stephenson, according to reports from PuckPedia. Sportsnet’s Elliotte Friedman notes the deal will have an approximate cap hit of $6.25 million.

This move marks Stephenson's departure from the Vegas Golden Knights, where he established himself as a key top-six center in the NHL. Stephenson originally joined Vegas in 2019 through a trade with the Washington Capitals, who received a 2021 fifth-round pick in return.

Stephenson made an immediate impact with Vegas, tallying 22 points in 41 games following the trade and contributing five points in 20 playoff appearances that season while serving as the team’s third-line center. His strong performance earned him a promotion to the second line in the 2020-21 season, where he set a then-career-high with 14 goals and 35 points in 51 games.

The following season, Stephenson continued his upward trajectory with a breakout performance of 21 goals and 64 points in 2021-22, followed by an impressive 65 points in 2022-23.

Stephenson solidified his reputation during the 2023 Stanley Cup Playoffs, playing a pivotal role in Vegas’ championship run with 20 points in 22 postseason games. He excelled as the second-line center, showcasing his defensive prowess and providing opportunities for his aggressive wingers.

Despite a slight dip in production this year, partly due to reduced ice time following Tomas Hertl’s acquisition, Stephenson remains known for his strong two-way play and aggressive net presence.

With his role in Vegas diminished by the arrivals of Hertl and William Karlsson, Stephenson is now poised to join the Kraken, a team brimming with opportunities.

Seattle faced lineup challenges last season, requiring top talents like Jared McCann and Yanni Gourde to fill multiple roles. Stephenson’s versatility will ease some of this burden and could potentially support McCann’s return to the wing, where he excelled with a career-high 40 goals and 70 points last season.

Stephenson will also provide mentorship for young talent like Matthew Beniers, who continues to develop his dynamic game after a slightly less productive season.

Seattle has been active in free agency, securing defenseman Brandon Montour on a seven-year deal alongside Stephenson. These signings leave the Kraken with approximately $10 million in remaining cap space, most likely earmarked for negotiations with restricted free agents Beniers and Eeli Tolvanen. While budget constraints are tight, there may still be room for one additional acquisition.
