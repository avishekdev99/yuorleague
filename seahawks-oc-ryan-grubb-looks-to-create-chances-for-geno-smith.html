Seahawks OC Ryan Grubb Looks to create chances for Geno Smith

The only direction for new Seahawks offensive coordinator Ryan Grubb is up, given Seattle's struggles in offensive stats last season.

In 2023, the Seahawks ranked No. 21 in total yards (323 per game) and No. 28 in rushing yards (93 per game). They were last in time of possession and No. 23 in red-zone conversion percentage. Their 21.4 points per game, ranking 17th in the league, was the lowest-scoring offense in the Pete Carroll era, which began in 2011.

Grubb's task this offseason is to create a more consistent and explosive offense, leveraging the talent of Pro Bowl receivers DK Metcalf and Tyler Lockett, last year's first-round pick Jaxon Smith-Njigba, and dynamic running backs Kenneth Walker III and Zach Charbonnet.

"We're trying to meld together some of the things we've done in the past, whether a long time ago or recently," Grubb said. "We want to be a physical, dominant team with an explosive, confusing element that people are used to."

After winning the Associated Press Comeback Player of the Year and earning a Pro Bowl nod in 2022, Geno Smith's production dipped last season. He completed 64.7% of his passes for 3,624 yards, with 20 touchdowns and nine interceptions. Injuries along the offensive line contributed to his inconsistent play, with the Seahawks using nine different offensive line combinations last season. Key tackles Charles Cross and Abe Lucas missed significant time due to injuries.

This season, the Seahawks expect to have three new starters on the interior offensive line: free agent addition Laken Tomlinson at left guard, second-year pro Olu Oluwatimi at center, and a competition at right guard among third-round pick Christian Haynes, incumbent Anthony Bradford, and prospect McClendon Curtis.

Grubb's challenge is to reignite the running game and create opportunities for Smith to quickly distribute the ball and execute deep passes. The Seahawks had just six passing plays of 40-plus yards last season, ranking 25th in the NFL.

Grubb, who previously led the Washington Huskies to the national title game with one of college football's most explosive offenses, aims to bring similar success to Seattle. Last season, Huskies quarterback Michael Penix Jr. led college football with 1,787 deep passing yards, as reported by Pro Football Focus.

Smith is eager to integrate these passing concepts into Seattle's offense. "I feel like I'm a drop-back passer, and this is a drop-back offense," Smith said. "An offense that's going to spread the ball around and trust the quarterbacks to make the right decisions."

Under Grubb, the Seahawks are likely to use more pre-snap shifts and motions to create mismatches. Last season, the Seahawks used pre-snap shifts or motions 48.6% of the time, ranking 21st in the league. In contrast, the Dolphins led the league with 80.3%, followed by the 49ers (79.2%) and the Rams (71.9%).

Grubb also intends to involve running backs more in the passing game. Last season, Seattle's backs had 68 receptions for 494 receiving yards and one touchdown, placing them among the bottom third in the league. "We want to make every guy on the field as dangerous as possible from any position," Grubb said.

Smith anticipates more offensive production in his third season as the team's starter. "There are going to be great things coming for us," Smith said. "Coach Grubb is doing a great job being demanding and ensuring guys are studying and on point, but also giving them some grace. It's a new system, and there will be mistakes. We can learn from them. Our job is to make the plays come alive and make it all look good."
