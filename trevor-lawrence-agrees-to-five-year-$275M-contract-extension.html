Trevor Lawrence Agrees to Five-Year $275M Contract Extension

Trevor Lawrence has secured a five-year, $275 million contract extension with the Jacksonville Jaguars, making him one of the highest-paid quarterbacks in NFL history. This new deal, which includes $142 million fully guaranteed and a $37.5 million signing bonus, ties Lawrence with Cincinnati Bengals quarterback Joe Burrow for the highest annual average salary in the NFL at $55 million.

This extension follows Detroit's recent four-year, $212 million deal with Jared Goff, who now ranks as the second-highest-paid quarterback with an average salary of $53 million. Lawrence joins an elite group of quarterbacks earning over $50 million annually, including Burrow, Goff, Justin Herbert (Los Angeles Chargers), Lamar Jackson (Baltimore Ravens), and Jalen Hurts (Philadelphia Eagles). At 24 years old, Lawrence is the youngest among the 14 quarterbacks earning more than $40 million per year.

Lawrence has started 52 games over three seasons, posting a 21-31 record and a playoff record of 1-1. During this time, he has accumulated 11,770 passing yards, with 58 touchdowns and 39 interceptions, while completing nearly 64 percent of his passes. He also has 11 rushing touchdowns and has lost 21 fumbles.

Despite an injury-riddled 2023 season, Jaguars' General Manager Trent Baalke and Head Coach Doug Pederson remain optimistic about Lawrence's potential. During a 19-game stretch spanning the 2022-23 seasons, Lawrence showcased his capabilities, throwing for 4,713 yards with 29 touchdowns and 11 interceptions, leading Jacksonville to a 14-5 record.

Lawrence's injury issues last season, including a sprained right shoulder, a sprained left knee, a sprained right ankle, and a concussion, contributed to a late-season collapse. The Jaguars, who were 9-8, lost five of their last six games, missing the playoffs after leading the AFC South for much of the season. Lawrence finished the season with 4,016 passing yards, 21 touchdowns, 14 interceptions, four rushing scores, seven fumbles lost, and was sacked 35 times.

To support Lawrence, Baalke revamped the receiving corps, replacing Calvin Ridley, Zay Jones, and Jamal Agnew with first-round pick Brian Thomas Jr., former Buffalo starter Gabe Davis, and Devin Duvernay.
